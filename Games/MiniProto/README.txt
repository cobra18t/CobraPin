Cut MiniProto3_layout out of 1/2" material. I used 1/2" particle board from Home Depot.

All 3D printed parts were printed from PETG. PLA can also work but is more likely to crack.

You need 1/4" thick straight pieces glued to the inside of the sides for the playfield to rest on. I used scrap material so it is not included in any drawings. The top of the 1/4" material should line up with the bottom edge of the 3 slots in the side pieces. When I cut those slots on my CNC, I only cut them ~0.010" deep since I was just using them as a reference line for placing these playfield supports.

I used WPC flippers from Pinball Life with 2" bats

I drove it with a CobraPin Satellite board.

The ball is a 3/4" ball bearing

The bolts for holding down the playfield parts were #6 x 2". Some of these were too long. You could use shorter one's in those cases, cut them down, or leave them not all the way screwed in.

1/4-20 bolts/nuts were used for levelers. I also printed feet that I found on Printables.com out of TPU.
